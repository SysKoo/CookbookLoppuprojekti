Chorizo wok

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
This chorizo wok might just be the easiest dinner I've ever made. The ease of cooking it doesn't take anything away from its taste though, as it's also one of my very favourite foods!

Ingredients:
(3-4 portions)

  • ~850g wok veggies (frozen)
  • ~230g chorizo sausage
  • Ketchup
  • Soy sauce
  • Example spices: black pepper, chili, onion powder, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Before starting you can take your wok out of the freezer to start thawing in room temperature, though this isn't necessary.
  2. Using a knife, cut the chorizo sausages into small bits, around the width of a finger or however big you prefer.
  3. Fry your cut up chorizo on the pan for a few minutes on medium-high heat, (no need to add oil, as the sausage is greasy enough) they will let out grease and change color slightly. They don't need to fry for long as theyre not raw and are going back on the pan later! Take out the fried chorizo and put it into a bowl to wait. You do not need to drain/remove any leftover grease, as it will give the wok a lot of flavor!
  4. Put your wok onto the pan, mix as well as possible to separate the pieces and leave under a lid to thaw in medium heat. You should mix it every few minutes, once you can cut the biggest pieces (in the example bag those would be the small corns) with your spatula the wok is thawed enough and you can move on to the next step.
  5. Add your chorizo back into the pan and add seasoning. I would do a big spiral of ketchup and soy sauce over the pan, and then add your preferred spices, or try the example ones.
  6. Mix well and let the wok sit in the pan for around 10 minutes or until you get too impatient to let it sit longer.
  7. Enjoy!

NOTE!

For a smaller meal (2 portions) you can buy the smaller version of the wok and either use only half of the chorizo or have the meal be more chorizo-heavy!
If you're not a fan of the spice in chorizo you can also make this recipe with normal sausage, or switch to something like chicken!

Example shopping list:

  • Findus Wok classic style 850g (pink bag)
  • Snellman all natural Chorizo grillimakkara 230g
  • Any ketchup, I use Heinz
  • Any soy sauce, I use Kikkoman

Tools:

  • Knife + cutting board
  • Frying pan
  • Spatula
  • Bowl

Tuna pasta

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
This tuna pasta is a big recipe, but it survives in the fridge for a long time and never loses its delicious taste. It was the 1st dinner I ever learned to make, and I still have it weekly.

Ingredients:
(4-5 portions)

  • 3 cans tuna in oil
  • 1 jar tomato sauce
  • 1 tub sour cream
  • 3 tablespoon sun dried tomato cream cheese
  • 1 vegetable fond
  • 4-5 portions of pasta of your choice
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Open the cans of tuna and drain most of the oil out. Two cans should be as dry as you can reasonably get them and one should have maybe half of the oil left.
  2. Empty the cans into your frying pan on medium-high heat and start separating the chunks with a spatula. The fish doesn't need to cook much, but I like to let it sizzle for a minute or two.
  3. Add in your tomato sauce, mix well and let simmer under the lid for two or three minutes.
  4. Lower the heat to low or medium-low and add in your sourcream and cream cheese and mix in well. Put a lid over the frying pan with a gap on one side to let steam out (this will let the evaporating water out, thickening the sauce) and leave to simmer.
  5. Cook your pasta now. Add a vegetable fond into the pastawater and mix it in white it boils to add flavor to the pasta. Keep the sauce simmering and mix every so often.
  6. A few minutes before your pasta is done, use a ladle to get around 1dl (not specific) of the pasta water and mix into your sauce. This will help the sauce not separate as much, as the starch in pasta water helps bind oils and water together. Put the lid back on with a gap to the side and let the sauce simmer for a few more minutes.
  7. When the pasta is done you can either mix it into the sauce in the pan or serve separately.
  8. Serve with some parmesan cheese on top and enjoy!

NOTE!

If you want a bit more spice, you can use the chili flavored Mutti sauce and add some parmesan cheese into the sauce after the cream and cream cheese!

Example shopping list:

  • Abba yellowfin tonnikalaa öljyssä (this is very good quality, worth the price)
  • Mutti pastakastike Parmigiano reggiano -juustolla (glass jar)
  • Kotimaista kermaviili 200g
  • Valio viola aurinkokuivattu tomaatti tuorejuusto 200g
  • Knorr Fond "du chef" Vegetable
  • Any pasta, I use Torino täysjyvä cappelli 500g
  • Coop Parmigiano Reggiano (block or pre-grated)

Tools:

  • Frying pan
  • Spatula
  • Big pot
  • Ladle

Bacon and ham pasta

Spoon score: 2 / 3 spoons.
This bacon and ham pasta has been an absolute hit with everyone I've made it for! It's easy and quick to make, and definitely delicious.

Ingredients:
(4-5 portions)

  • ~170g bacon
  • ~200g ham
  • 4dl cream
  • 1 vegetable fond
  • 4-5 portions of pasta of your choice
  • Example spices: black pepper, white pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Start by cutting up the meats: you need a knife, cutting board and clean kitchen scissors (only a knife works too). Take about 2/3 of your ham and cut it into small cubes with a knife. I've found it helps to separate the pieces slightly with your hands after cutting, as the ham likes to stick together.
  2. Next take the bacon strips out of the package one by one to separate them, I like to lay them out in three piles. Then, using either your knife or scissors (scissors are noticeably easier) you will cut up the bacon into small-ish bits. You can experiment with size here too, see an example below:



  3. Next fry your bacon bits on medium-high heat until they change color and don't look raw anymore (no need to add oil, as bacon is greasy enough), don't fry them all the way dark/crispy. Next add the ham and keep frying for a few more minutes, the ham isn't raw, but a bit of frying makes the taste better.
  4. Add in your cream and spices and mix well and lower to medium-low heat. Put a lid over the frying pan with a gap on one side to let steam out (this will let the evaporating water out, thickening the sauce).
  5. Cook your pasta now, add a vegetable fond into the pastawater and mix it in white it boils to add flavor to the pasta. Keep the sauce simmering and mix every so often.
  6. Once the pasta is done, add it into the pan and mix well. Most of the sauce will soak into the pasta and disappear, that is supposed to happen.
  7. Serve with some parmesan cheese on top and enjoy!

Example shopping list:

  • HK Amerikan pekoni 170g
  • Kivikylän savumaukas maalaispalvikinkku 250g
  • 2x Valio keittiön ruokakerma 2dl
  • Knorr Fond "du chef" Vegetable
  • Any pasta, I use Torino täysjyvä cappelli 500g
  • Coop Parmigiano Reggiano (block or pre-grated)

Tools:

  • Knife + cutting board
  • Kitchen scissors (optional but very helpful)
  • Frying pan
  • Spatula
  • Pot
