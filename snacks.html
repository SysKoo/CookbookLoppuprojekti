Overnight oats

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
These fiber-rich overnight oats make a perfect healthy breakfast or lunch and can be flavored with just about anything you can think of adding!

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • 3 tablespoons rolled oats (fin. kaurahiutale)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 3-4 tablespoons plain youghurt
  • 5 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon jam (any flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Put 3 tablespoons of oats and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds into your bowl.
  2. Add in 3-4 tablespoons of plain yoghurt and mix well. The amount of youghurt affects how thick/runny the finished oats will be, so experiment with what you like!
  3. Add in 5 tablsepoons of cold water from the tap and mix. Be very careful here, I've had water splash all over the counter too many times to count.
  4. Add in ~1 tablespoon of jam. You can experiment with the amount and flavor of your jam, or try another flavoring alltogether!
  5. Mix together well, cover with a lid/plastic/foil and place into the fridge overnight.
  6. Wait until the next morning, or whenever you want to eat and enjoy!

NOTE!

Apart from different jams, you can also use things like mashed bananas or other fruit/berries, honey, cocoa powder or nutella to flavor your oats. Really anything you can think of is worth a try!

Tools:

  • Small-medium bowl or cup (I recommend a glass bowl with a lid)
  • Plastic wrap or foil IF using a bowl without lid
  • 2 tablespoons

Egg butter and karelian pies

Spoon score: 2 / 3 spoons.
Make egg butter on top of karelian pies warmed in the oven or toaster for a filling snack or combine with spinach soup from the freezer-isle for a full, yet super easy to make meal! The recipe is for one pie's worth, so multiply by however many you plan to make!

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • sprinkle of salt
  • 1 karelian pie

Instructions:

  1. First you need to boil eggs, I usually do one egg per karelian pie. Put water into a small pot (test by placing an egg into the water, if it's submerged you have enough). Put the water to boil WITHOUT EGGS and add a bit of salt to prevent an egg from spreading everywhere if it's shell cracks.
  2. Once the water is boiling add your eggs in carefully with a spoon to make sure they don't break when hitting the bottom of the pot. Leave to boil for 10 minutes to get the inside fully cooked.
  3. When 10 minutes is up, take the pot off of the stove and pour out the water. Then pour cold water into your pot and either leave the eggs to cool in it for a few minutes, or continuously pour out and re-fill with cold water until the eggs and pot feel cool to the touch. This "shocks" the eggs and makes them easier to peel!
  4. Next, peel your eggs. Place them onto a large plate mash them slightly with a fork so adding the butter will be easier.
  5. Now add between 0,5 to 1 tablespoons of butter per egg, so for 4 eggs you'd add 2-4 spoonfuls. The amount varies by taste, but I recommend starting with less and adding more after.
  6. Use the fork to mash the butter and eggs together until satisfied with the consistency. Add some salt and mix well.
  7. Enjoy!

NOTE!

You could also boil the eggs by adding them into the cold, salted water instead of waiting for the pot to boil. With this method the boil time of the egg will vary since different stoves heat up faster than others.

  • Any butter, I use Valio Oivariini vähemmän suolaa
  • Fazer Imatran riisipiirakka

Tools:

  • Small pot
  • Tablespoon
  • Plate
  • Fork

Sausage oven toasts

Spoon score: 2 / 3 spoons.
These oven toasts are one of my go-to evening snacks on weekends, they're easy to make, delicious and still work wonders microwaved the next morning. This recipe doesn't need specific ingredient amounts, make as many as you want.

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • Toast
  • Butter
  • Sausage
  • Cheese
  • Example spices: smoked paprika, oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 225°C
  2. Place as many pieces of toast as you'd like to make onto an oven tray. Butter the toasts, but avoid the middle as it may get too soft if too much grease is on it.
  3. Take the peel off of your sausage (if it has one) and start cutting it into thin slices (around one finger wide, or however thick you prefer). Place four slices onto each toast.
  4. On top of the sausage slices sprinkle some smoked paprika, then add shredded cheese, then some oregano on top of that.
  5. Put the toasts into the oven for about 10-15 minutes or until the cheese starts to turn brown.
  6. The toasts are ready right out of the oven, enjoy!

NOTE!

You can make these with just about any other toppings you can think of! Make them more pizza-like with some tomato sauce or salsa under your other toppings, or spread on leftover pasta sauce (especially bolognese) to save on waste!

  • Any butter, I use Valio Oivariini vähemmän suolaa
  • HK Sininen lenkki
  • Coop mozzarella juustoraaste 150g

Tools:

  • Knife + cutting board
  • Oven tray + parchment paper

Tortilla mini-pizzas

Spoon score: 3 / 3 spoons.
These tortilla pizzas are a perfect fun snack for hosting a night in or when you just want to spend time making a slightly more special snack! This recipe doesn't need specific ingredient amounts, make as many as you want.

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • Tortillas
  • Salsa
  • Pepperoni
  • Ham
  • Canned pineapple
  • Grated cheese
  • Oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 225°C.
  2. First you prep the pepperonis by frying out some of the extra grease. Take out 12 pepperoni slices per pizza and place them on a frying pan. Let the pepperonis sit in the pan while it heats up, moving them around a bit with a spatula. You'll know they're done once they have changed color and gotten noticeably smaller.
  3. Take the pepperonis out of the pan and put them into a bowl with some paper towels laid inside. This will soak up even more grease. Now extra grease won't come out in the oven and make your tortilla soggy.
  4. Take out your other ingredients, cut up the ham into small chunks and drain the juice out of the pineapple cans.
  5. Lay out your tortillas. Use a spoon to spread a thin layer of salsa all over them, don't be scared of going right up to the edge of the tortilla.
  6. Put on your toppings. I usually start with a bit of cheese, then the ham and pineapple, then more cheese and top off with pepperoni and oregano. Make sure to leave the very center of the tortilla empty. (I usually lay out 4 pepperonis in a square in the middle, and the rest in a circle around those, this way each piece gets the same amount.)
  7. Bake in the oven for around 5-10 minutes or until the cheese has melted and the pizza has a golden brown colour (bake-times can vary quite a bit, as some ingredients are delicate).
  8. Cut into four slices and enjoy!

NOTE!

If you want to save some money on cheese, you can buy a whole block and grate it yourself, it's ridiculously cheap for the amount of grated cheese you get doing this!
These topping are just what I like, but you can put on whatever your heart desires! (And no, there will be no pineapple on pizza discourse.)
