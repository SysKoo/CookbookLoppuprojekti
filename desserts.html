Crispy oatmeal cookies

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
These thin and crispy oatmeal cookies won't even take you 30 minutes to make, require no whisking or whipping of any kind, and are still delightfully good!

Ingredients:
(around 20 cookies)

  • 2dl rolled oats
  • 1dl sugar
  • 0,5 teaspoon vanilla sugar
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 50g butter
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C.
  2. Add all the dry ingredients (oats, sugars, flour, baking powder) into a large bowl and mix with a tablespoon.
  3. Melt the butter and let it cool down a bit, add to the dry ingredients.
  4. Add the egg and mix well.
  5. Take out an oven tray with parchment paper and use the mixing spoon to put small piles of dough onto the tray. The spoon shouldn't be quite full. Leave some space in between the piles as the cookies spread and flatten out in the oven.
  6. Bake on the middle level of the oven for about 7 minutes or until the cookies start browning.
  7. Let the cookies sit for a bit after baking, they will become crispy as they cool down.
  8. Enjoy!

NOTE!

Melt some chocolate in a bowl and drip over the cookies with a spoon for a fancy but easy to make decoration!

White chocolate & raspberry cheesecake

Spoon score: 3 / 3 spoons.
Non-bake cheesecakes are my very favourite "nicer" dessert to make! The amount of steps may seem daunting at first, but making these is really very easy once you've made the first one, and they're always worth the time spent.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Digestive cookies
  • 100g butter
  • 7 gelatin leaves
  • 250g quark (fin. maitorahka)
  • 400g unflavored cream cheese
  • 2dl sugar
  • 4dl heavy cream
  • 300g raspberries
  • 200g white chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Start by lining your cake pan with parchment paper. For the bottom you can just take off the side of the pan, place a piece of parchment paper over the bottom and then lock the edge back on. For the side you should cut a strip of parchment paper the width of the pan's side. Rub a bit of butter or oil on the inside of the pan so the paper sticks to it.
  2. If you're using frozen raspberries instead of fresh ones, take them out to thaw. You can thaw them faster by putting the bag into warm water in the sink or a bowl/pot of some kind.
  3. Next take the cookies and crush them up as well as you can. I've found the best way to do this is to place the cookies in a minigrip-bag and use a heavy glass bottle or rolling pin over it.
  4. Melt the butter in the microwave and mix with the crushed up cookies.
  5. Take the lined cake pan and put the cookie-butter mixture inside, press it flat into the bottom, and then move the pan into the fridge to wait.

  6. Place 3 gelatin leaves into a small bowl of cold water to soak.
  7. In a large bowl, mix together half of the sugar (1dl), cream cheese (200g) and quark (125g), this will become the first one of two different fillings.
  8. Melt the white chocolate in the microwave in a microwave safe bowl, being careful to not burn it, then leave it to cool down.
  9. Take half of your heavy cream (2dl) and whip it with an electric mixer in a separate bowl.
  10. Get about 2 full tablespoons of water and warm it up on the stove or in the microwave. Take the gelatin leaves out of the cold water, squeeze out extra water from them and dissolve them in the hot water.
  11. Whisk the water-gelatin mixture into the large bowl with your sugar, cheese and quark, then mix in the melted chocolate.
  12. Using a spatula, carefully mix the whipped cream into the large bowl.
  13. Take the cake pan out of the fridge and scoop the white chocolate filling into it, using the spatula to smooth it out. Place the cake pan into the fridge.

  14. Take the remaining 4 gelatin leaves and place them into cold water to soak.
  15. Put the raspberries and the rest of the sugar into a small pot, mix together and briefly boil them on the stove.
  16. Press the raspberry-sugar mixture through a strainer so it becomes a smooth "puree". Take the gelatin leaves, squeeze out the extra water and dissolve into the warm berry puree.
  17. Take the rest of the heavy cream and whip it in the same bowl as before.
  18. Mix the rest of the quark and cream cheese into the berry-gelatin mixture in a large bowl (rinse out and use the one from before), then fold and mix in the whipped cream.
  19. Take the cake pan out of the fridge and pour the raspberry filling inside, smoothe it out with a spatula.
  20. Put the cake pan into the fride to set for a minimum of four hours, preferrably overnight for the best result.
  21. After the wait: enjoy!

NOTE!

I've found that these basic no-bake cheesecake recipes all follow the same methods. Just make this one once, maybe for some special occasion, and you'll pretty much know how to make any and all similar ones!

Chocolate chip cookies

Spoon score: 3 / 3 spoons.
This recipe takes some time and effort, but I can guarantee it is more than just worth your while. I've made these more times than I can count and am yet to find a person who doesn't find these absolutely heavenly.

Ingredients:
(around 35 cookies)

  • 270g flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 225g unsalted butter
  • 200g brown sugar
  • 100g sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 yolk
  • 1,5 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 250g chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Start by prepping the chocolate chips. On a cutting board, use a sharp knife to cut cold chocolate into chunks. If you're using a bar with squares I recommend cutting each individual square in a grid-pattern. There will be a lot of chocolate shavings alongside the chunks, keep those too.
  2. In a large bowl mix the flour, baking soda, cornstarch and salt.
  3. In a second large bowl whisk together melted butter and both the brown and white sugars. Once the mixture is smooth whisk in the egg, the yolk and the vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients (step 2) into the dry ingredients in the first bowl and mix together well. Then mix in your chocolate chips.
  5. Use a spoon to take small balls of dough and place them onto a plate with parchment paper on it (this makes it so the dough balls don't stick to the plate later). Put the dough balls into the fridge for about 2 hours.
  6. After refridgerating, bake your dough balls on the middle level of the oven in 175°C for about 10-15 minutes or until the edges of the cookies start to brown. If you want the cookies to be soft in the middle keep the bake time closer to 10 minutes, or keep them in for the full 15 for crunchy ones.
  7. Enjoy!

NOTE!

The recipe makes a lot of cookies, so unless you're hosting a party or giving some away, I recommend placing half of the refridgerated dough balls in the freezer. The cookies get a bit dry after a few days, but the dough lasts around 3 months frozen and can be cooked right out of the freezer! I always have a small stockpile in my freezer so I can bake a few cookies if a craving arises.
At step 4 the dough is going to be VERY thick and will feel like it's fighting back against you mixing it, this is normal.
