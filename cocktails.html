Screwdriver

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
I don't think I've ever come accross an easier, more budget friendly cocktail. For the last 3 summers this thing has been my very best friend on those weekends where all I want to do is lounge around with a drink or pregame for an event!

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • Vodka
  • Orange juice
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. Drop a few ice cubes into your glass, so the drink doesn't warm up too much.
  2. Use a shotglass to measure out 1-2 shots of vodka into the glass, or just pour from the heart.
  3. Add orange juice and stir together with a spoon.
  4. Easy as that, enjoy!

NOTE!

You can experiment with using flavored vodkas, or maybe adding in a dash of some other drink, like limoncello or sprite, for a different taste! Decorate with a slice of any citrus fruit for a nicer look.

Example shopping list:

  • Finlandia vodka
  • any orange juice, I usually use Xtra for this because its budget friendly and tasty in a cocktail.

Tools:

  • Shotglass (optional)
  • Glass
  • Spoon

Baileys caramel latte (starbucks style)

Spoon score: 1 / 3 spoons.
If you're craving a big sugarbomb of a sweet drink, this baileys caramel latte is the drink to make!

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • Caramel sauce
  • Baileys original
  • Premade latte drink
  • Whipped cream

Instructions:

  1. First take your glass, tilt it slightly and drizzle caramel sauce stripes all around the sides, you can also pour a small amount into the bottom to mix into your drink if you want to.
  2. Use a shotglass to measure out 1-2 shots of Baileys into the glass, or just pour from the heart.
  3. Fill the rest of the glass with the ready made latte. Mix well with a spoon, while trying not to touch the sides of the glass if you want to keep the caramelly design.
  4. Spray whipped cream on top of the drink, and then drizzle on a bit more caramel sauce to decorate.
  5. Enjoy!

NOTE!

If you happen to have chocolate in your house, use a cheesegrater to grate some on top of the drink as a last step for a nice little decoration.

Example shopping list:

  • O'hoj jälkiruokakastike kinuski
  • Baileys original
  • Valio latte original maitokahvijuoma
  • Cessibon 250g spraykermavaahto

Tools:

  • Shotglass (optional)
  • Glass
  • Spoon

Mint hot chocolate

Spoon score: 2 / 3 spoons.
The mint hot chocolate is my very favourite winter drink, and one of the main reasons I'm always looking forward to the next winter just a little bit!

Ingredients:
(one portion)

  • 3dl milk
  • 100g dark chocolate
  • 1tbsp sugar
  • Mint liquor
  • Whipped cream
  • Mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Put the milk, chocolate and sugar into a small pot and heat up on the stove. Mix with a spatula or whisk every minute or two, while making sure the milk doesn't boil or burn.
  2. Once the chocolate and sugar have sompletely dissolved, take the pot off the stove and pour the chocolate milk into a large cup.
  3. Add one shot of mint liquor and mix it in with a spoon.
  4. Spray whipped cream on top of the drink, and then sprinkle on some mini marshmallows. You can also only use one or the other if you want to.
  5. Enjoy!

NOTE!

This also works stunningly well with the salmiakki flavored mint liquor! You could also experiment with different chocolates, I've been wanting to try putting some Pätkis bites in with the dark chocolate.

Example shopping list:

  • Any milk, I use Valio Eila kevytmaitojuoma laktoositon
  • Brunberg tumma suklaa laktoositon 150g
  • AITO hienosokeri 1kg
  • Minttu peppermint likööri
  • Cessibon 250g spraykermavaahto
  • Kindly's Baking mini vaahtokarkit 150g

Tools:

  • Small pot
  • Spatula or whisk
  • Cup
  • Shotglass
  • Spoon
